Cytoskeleton

The cytoskeleton is a network of protein fibers that shape the cell. The cytoskeleton is made out of 3 main fibers

  • microfillaments
  • microtubules
  • intermediate filaments

Microfillaments

Microfillaments are long fibers, with are two protein chains intertwined together. Each subunit on the chain is made out of a protein called actin, which is where microfillaments get their second name, actin filaments. microfillaments have polar ends, which designate the growth of the filament itself.

Microtubules

Microtubules are the largest of the 3 elements, composed of a ring of 13 protein protofilaments. The filaments are arrayed side by side around a core, giving it it's tube shape. Microtubules are constantly polymerizing and depolymerizing, having an average half life of about 10 minutes. Microtubules facilitate cellular movement, organize the cytoplasm, and move materials around the cell.

Intermediate Filaments

Intermediate Filaments are the most durable element of the cytoskeleton, which are tough protein twined together like a mesh. They are in between microtubules and microfillaments in terms of size, and are the most common in a cell. They can provide structural stability within the cell, structural stability in the hair and fingernails, and even the filaments in nerve cells.

The cytoskeleton does a lot for the cell, but one of those functions is to move material within the cell. It is solely responsible for shape and movement, but also helps with the rough ER. Microfillaments contract muscles, and enzymes involved in cell metabolism bind to the microfillaments. In the cell, a vesicle moves along the microtubule with the help of a motor protein, and a connector molecule connecting the vesicle into the motor protein. The binding protein is the kinesin, and the motor protein is known as the dynein.

The arrangement of the microfillaments allow cells to physically crawl, one of the cellular phenomena, allowing cells like white blood cells to crawl and kill pathogens. The movement is throught the protein myosin, which contracts and pulls the cell towards itself. Eurkaryotes also have flagella, it consists of nine microtubule pairs, surround two central micortubules. The pairs of microtubules move past each other, waving up and down. Many eurkaryotes cannot be found with flagella, and are instead found with cilia. They are more numerous than flagella and have the same internal structure. Cilia moves water and other waves over the cell, and can even be attributed to hearing.
