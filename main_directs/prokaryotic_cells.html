Prokaryotic Cells

Prokaryotes have two domains, Archaea, and bacteria. Some of these prokaryotes harvest light with photosynthesis, and some break down dead animals. The defining trait of a absence of a membrane-bound nucleus. The DNA is instead contained in a nucleoid. They are simple cells that consist of a plasma membrane containing cytoplasm with genetic material and ribosomes. All of this is then encased by a rigid cell wall.
