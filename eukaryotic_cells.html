Eukaryotic Cells

Eukaryotic cells are larger and more complicated than prokaryotic cells. They have a complex endomembrane system that weaves through the cell, containing special compartments. Plants often have a central vacoule. Plants and animals both have cells called vesicles, which act as storage that transports. Inside the nucleus, chromosomes are dna tightly wound around proteins. All Eukaryotic cells are suppposed by the cytoskeleton, which acts like a scaffold. Animals and some protists do not have cell Walls, plants have strong ones made from cellulose, and fungi have cell walls made of chitin. The largest part of a cell is known as the nucleus, whihch contains the genetic information that lets ribosomes synthesize nearly all protein.
